Community Connections

As a member of many local chapters on elderly affairs, patient care groups, educational studies, and resource networks, our company remains dedicated to giving back to our community. We not only support these non-profit organizations, we offer programs like Pay It Forward to help individuals help others.

As longstanding business owners of our community, we feel we have a responsibility to help those that are less fortunate and contribute to the common good. We feel this so strongly that it's been a longstanding practice of our company to contribute our time, our resources and our services to these non-profit charitable organizations.

If you need help or are looking for great non-profit organization to get involved with in your community, here are just a few in the Fort Lauderdale, Broward and Palm Beach Counties.

Volunteer groups and non-profit organizations are a vital part of our community. In order to continue to provide help to those in need, they can't do it alone. They need your help!





We’d like to introduce you to four charities where we serve as volunteers:


1. Area Agency on Aging
Advisory Council Members – Fund Raising Co-Chair

The Area Agency on Aging plans, coordinates, and evaluates programs; funds services; and is the prime advocate for Broward County residents 60 years of age or older. Named Volunteer of the Year of Broward County from ADRC.

www.adrcbroward.org
Make a Donation


2. Senior Volunteer Services
Advisory Council Chair – Fund Raising Committee

Senior Volunteer Services is the premier volunteer service organization in Broward County, Florida for older adults who want to remain active, healthy, and involved in their communities by contributing time to improve the lives of others.

www.seniorvolunteerservices.org
Make a Donation


3. Florida’s Voice for Developmental Disabilities
Board Member

Florida’s Voice on Developmental Disabilities (FVDD) is a statewide independent advocacy and networking non-profit organization comprised of families and friends of persons with developmental disabilities. Founded in 1995 in Fort Lauderdale, Broward County, Florida, FVDD was created to be a statewide voice for families and friends of persons with developmental disabilities throughout Florida.

Make a donation


4. Alzheimer's Association SEFC
Longstanding Supporter in Fund Raising and Community Education

The Southeast Florida Alzheimer’s Chapter enhances efforts for researching a cure and provides support and educational services to over 200,000 Alzheimer's patients, families and caregivers in Broward, Palm Beach, Miami-Dade, Martin, Monroe, St. Lucie and Okeechobee counties.

www.alz.org/seflorida
Make a donation
